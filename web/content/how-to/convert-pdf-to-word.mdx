## Conversion Quality Tips

**Best Results**: PDFs created from Word or similar tools convert with near-perfect fidelity.

**Scanned Documents**: Enable OCR for scanned PDFs. Quality depends on scan clarity. Use 300 DPI scans for best results.

**Complex Layouts**: Documents with multiple columns, text boxes, and wrapped images may require manual adjustment after conversion.

**Forms**: PDF forms convert to tables or text. You may need to recreate form fields in Word.

**Fonts**: Embedded fonts convert correctly. Non-embedded fonts substitute with similar alternatives.

## Post-Conversion Editing

**Review Formatting**: Check fonts, spacing, and alignment. Make minor adjustments as needed.

**Verify Tables**: Ensure table structure converted correctly. Merge or split cells if necessary.

**Check Images**: Confirm images positioned correctly and at acceptable quality.

**Test Hyperlinks**: Verify internal and external links work as expected.

**Proofread OCR**: For scanned documents, review OCR accuracy and correct any recognition errors.

## When NOT to Convert

**Final Documents**: If the PDF is the final version for distribution, keep it as PDF for universal compatibility.

**Legal Documents**: Signed PDFs with digital signatures lose those signatures when converted.

**Complex Graphics**: PDFs with sophisticated vector graphics may not convert perfectly to Word.

**Large Files**: Very large PDFs (500+ pages) may be easier to edit as PDFs with specialized tools.
